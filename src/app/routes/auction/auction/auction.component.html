Artist Biography

Michael Cheval was born in 1966 in Kotelnikovo, a small town in southern Russia. He grew up among paints and brushes, canvases and easels. His grandfather, a professional artist and sculptor, developed Michael?s love for drawing in his early childhood. In 1980, Michael moved with his family to Germany. His new setting made a great impression on the young artist. Museums and castles, ancient streets and wonderful landscapes of southern Germany permanently defined Michael?s tastes. After moving to Turkmenistan in 1886, he took formal training at the Ashgabad School of Fine Arts and focused on his lifelong style of what some would call surrealism, but which he calls ?Absurdism.? He considers absurdity as a game of imagination, where all ties are chosen to construct a literary plot. His work includes metaphorical references and the viewer is challenged to find the often hidden allusions. His decision to immigrate in 1997 to United States began a productive era for Michael. He returned to the Western culture that greatly inspired him in his youth, but now he brought his own experience, his philosophy and vision.
